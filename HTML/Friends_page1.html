The Situation Now.

Our world is advancing rapidly even compared to the explosive growth after the devastating WW2. The new generation, nicknamed "tech natives", are developing in a whole new world than anyone can truly prepare them for. Meanwhile, the older generations have a hard time catching up in this brave new world. Therefore it begs the question, how good are these changes?. In this page I will summarize the results I have found into short concise answers so that young and old alike can easily read and comprehend my content.


I will ask for the future readers to continue where I left off and read more about this topic as no one perspective can truly encapsulate a topic as large as this. I will be leaving my sources at the bottom of the page for where I got my thoughts. Now off you go to scroll down and read :>.

The Good
&
The Bad

It grants easy communication to people all around the world allowing for Long-Distance Friendships to occur, unlikely friends to meet, and most importantly to contact friends to talk/hangout!

It allows for multiple conversations to happen. This is most commonly seen with messaging centered apps like messenger. This is a great addition as it allows for efficient use of time. In work, family, or friendships.



It allows for people to reach out to the world for help. In can be in the form of a google search to search for how to do a task, A reddit post to ask for advice, or a blog to spread awareness of a personal emergency. All of this allows people, often strangers, to see their plight and help them solve it.



The disconnect allows people to easily express their thoughts and emotions to their friends or to the world. ICT also allows them to express it in pictures and video, things not easily done otherwise.



The disconnect also allows shy people to open up. This is sometimes the only way people can be "eased" in to social life. A much better alternative to "Go out and make friends".

People can grow reliant on the detachedness of technology leading to underdeveloped face to face social skills. This is currently a very real and widespread problem.



ICT may give you easy access to the people you cherish in life but it also gives easy access to the people you want to keep away from to you. Cyberbullying is a very real thing as well as a plethora of insults by the online community if you manage to gain the ire of a crowd.



Isolation, a state that could be further amplified by ICT, after all whats worse than feeling lonely despite being surrounded by friends, online or face to face.



Vanity, a sin that is being fed by ICT. One can spend hours just to get the picture just right or to get a post just right to feed their ego. On the other side however, it could also pressure people in putting up a front to hide what they feel or think as they won't want to be left behind.



Access to too much information, We all know that a lot of people have seen their first look of another person's... for a lack of a better term uncovered body or other inappropriate subjects in this internet world.



Do you know the saying where one shows his true self when no one is looking? The Anonimity of the internet allows people to freely express their true selves without repercussion, the good AND the bad

Conclusion

ICT has many good and bad effects. Many studies will agree with me that its for the better but not without its risks. It's easily one of the best tools one person can have. It is an amplifier of anything and everything that you focus on while using it. It can help you connect with others, better yourself, and stand up for your values when you use it right but it can be easily be excessive and sometimes addictive, the fame , the pressure, the physical isolation of it all could easily be your worst enemy. Just be true to yourself, stand with your friends, always do whats right, and ICT will do the rest.
